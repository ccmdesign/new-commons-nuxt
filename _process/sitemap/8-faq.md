## why-apply

title: Why should I apply for the Incubator?
items:
  - Join a first-of-its kind incubator for the next generation of data commons
  - Become part of an international community of linguistic and cultural data commons leaders
  - Learn emerging governance models and gain access to technical infrastructure and expertise
  - Receive mentorship from international experts
  - Share your work with global funders through the final showcase

## evaluation

title: How will you evaluate submissions?
tagline: Our jury will evaluate submissions based on the following criteria.
items:
  - Opportunity (30%): What's the opportunity?
  - Relevance (20%): How does the data commons support an Indigenous community?
  - Capacity (20%): What assets do you have available to manage this project?
  - Governance (20%): How will this effort be commonly governed?
  - Team (10%): Who is available to participate in the Incubator?
note: More details can be found in the application form.

## concept-readiness

title: How far along does my data commons concept need to be?
body: |
  Concepts submitted to the Incubator can be in the early stages but must be thought out enough so that a proposal can be completed and far enough along that there is a commitment from your organization for its implementation.

  Some concepts may be brand new initiatives whereas others may be extensions of existing products or programs.

## data-literacy

title: How much data literacy is required of incubator participants?
body: Participants in this program are not expected to be data scientists or have a background in data science. Applicants should know enough about data that they can articulate and understand the theory of change of their data innovation.

## funding

title: Do Incubator participants receive funding?
body: Incubator participants will have access to a variety of resources such as XYZ and the opportunity to present their project to global funders. Funding to implement the project is not part of the Incubator's offerings.

## time-commitment

title: What is the expected time commitment?
body: The Incubator will run programming for X weeks. It will include an X day in-person kickoff followed by weekly 2-hour programming for 4 months. At its conclusion, participants will present their work at our showcase. Participants should expect to complete approximately X hours of work outside of the formal programming hours.

## language

title: What language will the programming be delivered in?
body: All programming will be delivered in English. There will be a public writing component of the programming, and if program participants would like they can publish this in their local language.

## scheduling

title: When will programming take place?
body: We plan to schedule all live engagements on a rotational schedule to accommodate the various timezones of program participants and will work with all participants to ensure that no one is consistently joining engagements during an inconvenient date or time.

## geographic-restrictions

title: Are there geographic restrictions?
body: |
  The Incubator is a global initiative that welcomes entries from organizations across continents. We hope to receive applicants from diverse regions and contexts.

  We welcome any entry, though we may be unable to deliver programming to certain entities and individuals who are subject to sanctions and other restrictions under U.S. law, such as those based in Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Russia, Syria, and the Crimea, Donestsk, and Luhansk regions of Ukraine.

## organization-types

title: What types of organizations can participate?
body: We feel this initiative may be most applicable to nonprofit organizations, government organizations, academic institutions, start-ups, and development organizations. For-profit entities submitting applications may be asked to undergo extra review to ensure their concept note aids Indigenous communities.

## letter-of-support

title: What is a "letter of support"?
body: A letter of support is any letter, on institutional letterhead, from a representative of an institution indicating that they are aware of the concept and their institution will support a representative's participation in the Incubator. It is our way of ensuring that a representative is able to participate in the full Incubator programming.

## submission-issues

title: What if I am unable to submit or access the application form?
body: If, for any reason, you are unable to submit the form, you may download a text version of the form here and submit your responses to the questions as a Word doc or PDF file to newcommons@opendatapolicylab.org.

## multiple-applications

title: Can my organization submit multiple applications?
body: Your organization may only submit one application. If you mistakenly submitted an application early and need to delete your submission before submitting a revision, please contact us at newcommons@opendatapolicylab.org.
